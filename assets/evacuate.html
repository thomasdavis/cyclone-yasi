Evacuation notice from Cairns Disaster Coordination Centre

Update 4 7AM 2/2/2011: Just updated evacuation listings on early this morning. You can't take pets, alcohol or weapons. Food is supplied at most of the shelters I could hear, but you're allowed to bring food and water.

UPDATE 3 : View Evacuation Map for Mossman to Innisfail

Update 2: Locations have been updated listen to ABC radio for more updates.

Update: Smithfield State High has been cancelled. Instead, Trinity Beach State School and Earlville Shopping Centre have been added. In total 7 evacuation centres will be set up for tomorrow morning.

Cairns Regional Council advises that for those residents who have no alternative for moving to higher ground, evacuation centres will be open at 6am Wednesday 2 February at the following locations:

Cairns

  • Redlynch State College shelter building , Jungara Road, Redlynch

    Register on site

  • James Cook University – student accommodation facility, McGregor Road, Smithfield - map

    Make sure you go to the accomodation center

  • Smithfield State High School
  • Trinity Beach State Primary School – Madang Street, Trinity Beach (this facility replaces Smithfield High School, which is no longer available)

    Smithfield High School still closed

  • Babinda RSL Hall, School Street, Babinda
  • Mossman Community Indoor Sports Centre, Front Street, Mossman
  • Woree State School, Riggs Street, Woree - map
  • Stockland Earville, Mulgrave Rd (opp Balaclava Rd) - map

    Go up the second floor and go through cinema entrance

Innisfail

  • Innisfail State College

Ingham

  • Ingham Showground Pavillion - Pets allowed!

Conditions at these locations are minimal and residents are encouraged to bring their own bedding, food and medications.

Residents are urged to seek shelter with relatives and friends, as using these centres should be a last resort.

At these centres no pets, alcohol or weapons of any description will be allowed.

Contact the Centre on 4044 3377. If emergency medical assistance is required, dial 000.

An evacuation advice has been issued for those residents in the ORANGE (and therefore RED) Zone. Residents in the ORANGE zone should commence evacuation to higher ground as soon as possible and should register with the Cyclone Yasi Evacuation Register hotline on 1300 993 191.
